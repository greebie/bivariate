'Bivariate' networks have two variables, often committees and their members. Because committees have many members and people can be members of different committees we can observe:

  • What committees are of general interest and which "unique".
  • The social structure formed by who joins what committee.
  • The density or sparseness of this structure. For example, do the members join multiple committees, or just a few?
  • What subclasses of members (eg. professions) join different committees?
